# Go-To-Market Plan

**Target User**
The exact target user is a "Head of Engineering" or "VP of Engineering" at a Series A/B startup (50–150 employees). At this stage, they’ve moved past the "buy whatever it takes to build fast" phase and are facing their first real board pressure about cash burn and runway. They manage the tool stack but are often unaware of exactly how many duplicate or suboptimal AI licenses are floating around across their product and engineering teams.

**The Trigger Moment**
What they do right before wanting this tool:
They are reviewing their monthly Brex or Ramp spend report, or preparing for a quarterly budget review with their CFO. They see "Anthropic", "OpenAI", and "Cursor" line items that have mysteriously tripled over the last two quarters and wonder, "Are we even using all of this?"

**Where They Hang Out**
- **Slack Communities:** Rands Leadership Slack (especially the `#leadership` and `#engineering-managers` channels), CTO School Slack.
- **Newsletters:** Pragmatic Engineer (Gergely Orosz) reader comments, TLDR Web Dev.
- **X Lists:** Lists focused on "Engineering Leadership" and "Tech Leads".

**First 100 Users in 30 Days ($0 Budget)**
1. **The "Roast My Spend" Offer (Day 1-7):** Cold outbound on LinkedIn targeting newly promoted VPs of Engineering at recently funded startups. The pitch isn't a tool; it's an offer: "We'll audit your AI spend in 2 minutes for free. Most Series A teams are wasting $1k+/mo." (Goal: 15 users).
2. **Founder WhatsApp/Slack Infiltration (Day 8-15):** I am part of several founder group chats (e.g., YC alumni groups, local tech hubs). I will drop the tool link with a provocative statement: "Just found out we were burning $800/mo on redundant ChatGPT and Claude seats. Built this to check if you are too." (Goal: 35 users).
3. **Engineering Blog "Takedown" Post (Day 16-30):** Write a highly opinionated post on Hacker News / Dev.to titled "Why your engineers don't need both Copilot and Cursor, and other AI money pits." The CTA at the bottom is the tool. (Goal: 50 users).

**The Unfair Distribution Channel**
Credex's existing network of startups who *almost* bought credits but churned or paused. We email them: "Even if you don't buy from us, stop overpaying retail. Use this to right-size your plans." This leverages an existing, warm list.

**Week-1 Traction Metrics**
- **Visitors:** 400
- **Audits Completed:** 80 (20% conversion from visit)
- **High-Savings Leads Captured:** 12 (15% of audits)
- **Consultations Booked:** 3
